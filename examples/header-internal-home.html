Our role

Through our advice and programs we aim to make communication technologies and the arts better for all Australians.

What we do

We help the communications sector stay competitive and innovative by providing advice, delivering programs and conducting research.

We also manage programs that encourage artists, support Australia’s cultural heritage and help everyone experience arts and culture.

Portfolio agencies we work with

We work closely with other Government agencies within the Communications portfolio to provide all Australians with information and services.

A portfolio is an area of responsibility for how Australia is run. The Communications portfolio is led by the Minister for Communications.

  • Australia Council for the Arts
    The Australian Government’s arts funding and advisory body.

  • Australia Post
    Providing reliable and affordable postal, retail, financial and travel services.

  • Australian Broadcasting corporation (ABC)
    Australia’s public broadcaster, including national and local television and radio programs.

  • Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA)
    Responsible for the regulation of broadcasting, the internet, radiocommunications and telecommunications.

  • Australian Film Television and Radio School (AFTRS)
    The leading institution for the education and training of creative talent.

  • Australian National Maritime Museum
    Australia’s national centre for maritime collections, exhibitions, research and archaeology.

  • Bundanon Trust
    Supporting artists and encouraging people to engage with the arts through education, exhibitions and performances.

  • Australia Business Arts Foundation (operating as Creative Partnerships Australia)
    Bringing artists, donors, businesses and arts organisations together to promote a more sustainable and vibrant arts sector.

  • The Museum of Australian Democracy
    Helping people to understand Australia’s social and political history.

  • National Film and Sound Archive
    Collecting, preserving and sharing our rich audio-visual heritage.

  • National Gallery of Australia (NGA)
    Australia’s national cultural institution for the visual arts.

  • National Library of Australia (NLA)
    Making sure that important documentary resources are collected, preserved and made available to everyone.

  • National Museum of Australia
    A social history museum exploring the land, nation and people of Australia.

  • National Portrait Gallery Australia (NPGA)
    The national collection of portraits of Australians reflects the breadth and energy of Australian culture and endeavour.

  • NBN Co Limited
    Delivering Australia’s first national, open access broadband network to all Australians.

  • Office of the Children’s e-Safety Commissioner
    Helping young people have safe, positive experiences online.

  • Screen Australia
    Funding the production of everything from feature films to documentaries, television drama, children’s programs and online series.

  • Special broadcasting service (SBS)
    Australia’s multicultural and multilingual broadcaster.
