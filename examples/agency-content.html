What we work on

We promote an innovative and competitive communications sector by undertaking analysis, providing advice and delivering programs.

We also manage programs that encourage excellence in art, support for cultural heritage and public access to arts and culture.

The benefits of working here

Working with us can give you a rewarding and exciting career.

For more details on any of the items below, please see our Enterprise agreement.

Location

We are centrally located in Canberra, with offices around the country.

A range of on-site facilities help staff to balance their work and personal needs including:

  • Carers’ rooms.
  • Access to new technology to support flexible work arrangements including laptops, Wi-Fi and remote network access.

Competitive remuneration package

We offer a good salary, generous leave conditions, vacation care and an attractive superannuation scheme.

Flexible working arrangements

We provide access to new technology to support flexible work arrangements (such as the ability to work off-site) as well as flexible working hours.

Wellbeing Program and social club

The Wellbeing Program includes seminars and activities that focus on the social, mental and physical health of employees. It includes:

  • free influenza vaccinations
  • a carer’s room
  • health seminars
  • quit smoking assistance
  • annual health checks
  • cooking demonstrations
  • physical activities such as lunchtime walking groups.

Learning and development

Our employees have opportunities for learning and development throughout their careers including:

  • an induction program for new employees
  • a core training program with short courses for business skills and knowledge
  • in-house capability development programs
  • development programs to build management and leadership skills
  • a staff job rotation program and portfolio agency secondment program to help our employees broaden their skills and knowledge
  • access to mentoring programs.

Study assistance

Studybank is our study assistance program. It helps our employees develop their work-related skills through access to:

  • up to 75 hours paid leave per semester for study
  • financial assistance of up to $5000 per calendar year as per the Enterprise Agreement.

Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

The EAP is a confidential, professional short-term counselling service. It is a free service for our employees and their immediate families.

Smoke-free workplace

As an employer, we have a duty of care to protect our staff from passive smoking while they are working. We provide financial assistance to staff and members of their immediate families who want to quit smoking. There are designated smoking areas.

Support for carers

We offer services to support carers including:

  • The Vacation Childcare Subsidisation Program—for children aged between five and 12 years. To qualify, children must attend a registered child care service during the school holidays.
  • Carers’ room—available for staff who need to take care of immediate family members in an emergency. It gives people an alternative to using leave. Conditions of use apply.

Eldercare

Our eldercare information kit contains information on eldercare issues and how to deal with them. The kit is a national resource that has information on all states and territories.

  • Enterprise Agreement pay scale
  • Enterprise Agreement s24(1) determination
