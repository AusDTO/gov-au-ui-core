Emergency numbers

In an emergency, call

  • 000

    Call 000 from any landline or mobile phone if you need to call the police, fire or ambulance services in an emergency.

    This number also works on some internet-based phone services.

From a mobile phone, call

  • 112

    You can call 112 from most mobile phones if you need to call the police, fire or ambulance services in an emergency.

    You can call this number when your mobile phone screen is locked or (on some phones) when the phone is switched off.

    You can also call 112 when overseas to contact the emergency services in many other countries.

If you have hearing or speech difficulties, call

  • 106

    Call 106 from a textphone (also called a teletypewriter - TTY).

    This is the text-based emergency number for people who are deaf or who have a hearing or speech impairment.

Other important numbers

Storm and flood assistance

  • 132 500

    Call 132 500 for storm and flood assistance.

    In the Northern Territory call 131 444.

  • 1800 333 000

    Call Crime Stoppers on 1800 333 000 to help solve a crime.

Making phone calls within Australia

Calling a landline phone

Australian landline phone numbers have 8 digits. To call a landline phone in a different state to yours, you will first need to dial 1 of the 2-digit area codes listed below.

Example

To call Parliament House in Canberra ACT, you would dial the area code and phone number: 026277 7111

State and territory area codes for landline numbers

  • Area Code 02

    • Locations
      • New South Wales: Sydney
      • Australian Capital Territory: Canberra
      • Note: some parts of regional New South Wales use the area codes 08 and 03

  • Area Code 03

    • Locations
      • Victoria: Melbourne
      • Tasmania: Hobart
      • Note: some parts of regional Victoria use the area codes 02

  • Area Code 07

    • Locations
      • Queensland: Brisbane

  • Area Code 08

    • Locations
      • Western Australia: Perth
      • South Australia: Adelaide
      • Northern Territory: Darwin

Calling a mobile phone

  • 04

    Mobile phone numbers in Australia have 10 digits starting with the numbers 04.

    Example

    0491 570 156

    You do not need to use an area code in front of mobile phone numbers.

Calling a satelite phone

  • 014

    Some people in remote parts of Australia use satellite phones instead of mobile or landline phones.

    Satellite phone numbers in Australia have 10 digits starting with the numbers 014.

    Example

    0149 970 156

    You do not need to use an area code in front of satellite phone numbers.

Calling overseas

Calling overseas from a landline

  • 0011

    To call a person or organisation in another country, you will first need to dial Australiaâ€™s country exit code.

    Example

    The phone number for the New Zealand parliament in Wellington is 04 817 9999. To call this number from Australia, you would dial:
    00 11 64 4 817 9999

    This is made up as follows:

    • International exit code

      0011 64 4 817 9999

    • Country code

      0011 64 4 817 9999

    • Area code (without 0)

      0011 64 4 817 9999

    • Phone number

      0011 64 4 817 9999

Calling overseas from a mobile phone

  • 0011

    When using a mobile phone, you can choose to replace 00 11 with the + symbol.

    Example

    To call the New Zealand parliament from an Australian mobile phone, you can dial:
    +64 4 817 9999

    This is made up as follows:

    + symbol

    + 64 4 817 9999

    Country code

    + 64 4 817 9999

    Area code (without 0)

    + 64 4 817 9999

    Phone number

    + 64 4 817 9999

Calling Australia from overseas

Free call, local rate and premium rate numbers

  • 18 and 1800

    Phone numbers starting with 1800 are free from landline phones. Rates from a mobile phone may be higher - check with your mobile provider.

  • 13 and 1300

    Phone numbers starting with 13 and 1300 are charged at a similar rate to local calls when dialled from a landline phone. Rates from a mobile phone may be higher - check with your mobile provider.

  • 19 and 19000

    Phone numbers starting with 19 and 190 are more expensive because they are charged at a premium rate.
